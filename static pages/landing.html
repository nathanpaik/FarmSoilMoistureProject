CLIMATE CHANGE IN A HUNGRY WORLD

Summers in Los Angeles are getting longer and hotter. We know that extreme heat affects human health; but do you ever wonder what all that heat does to the food we grow? What can we do locally to mitigate the effects of climate change in our food gardens and yards? We can start by "growing healthy soil."

Climate Change in a Hungry World is a new effort to promote healthy soil practices in schools, the community, and other gardens and farms around Los Angeles. Some of these practises include the use of cover crops, mulch, native pollinator plants, and integrative technology.

We start by growing a cover crop to boost the yield and the nutritional value of the crops. We plan to integrate technology - a soil moisture monitor and weather station - to target irrigation and save water. Our goal is to add more gardens and establish a local Citizen Science Healthy Soils Network that will help us work together to address local challenges caused by climate change.
